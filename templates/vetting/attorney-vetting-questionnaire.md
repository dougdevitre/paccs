# Attorney Vetting Questionnaire

> **Audience:** Parents
> **Purpose:** Provide a structured set of questions to help parents evaluate whether an attorney has the knowledge, experience, and approach needed to effectively handle a parental alienation case.
> **Related Skill:** paccs-tech-advocate

---

## How to Use This Template

- Bring this questionnaire to your initial consultation with a prospective attorney. Most attorneys offer a free or low-cost initial consultation.
- Ask the questions in order. The first few questions are designed to quickly screen out attorneys who are not a good fit.
- Take notes on the attorney's responses and use the green flags / red flags to evaluate their answers.
- Interview at least two or three attorneys before making a decision, if possible.
- Trust your instincts. If something feels wrong, keep looking.

## Tips

- The most important question is #1. If an attorney does not acknowledge parental alienation as a real dynamic, they will not fight effectively for you. Move on.
- An attorney who has never handled an alienation case but is genuinely willing to learn and has strong family law experience may be acceptable if no alienation-experienced attorneys are available in your area.
- Ask for references from past clients in alienation cases, if the attorney is willing to provide them (some cannot due to confidentiality).
- Cost matters, but the cheapest attorney is not always the best value. An attorney who understands alienation may resolve your case faster and more effectively.
- If you cannot afford an attorney, explore legal aid (lawhelp.org), your local bar association's pro bono programs, and law school family law clinics.
