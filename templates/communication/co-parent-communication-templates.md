# Co-Parent Communication Templates

> **Audience:** Parents
> **Purpose:** Provide ready-to-use, court-friendly communication templates for five common co-parenting scenarios, using the BIFF method (Brief, Informative, Friendly, Firm).
> **Related Skill:** paccs-tech-advocate

---

## How to Use This Template

- Choose the scenario that matches your situation and customize the bracketed fields.
- Follow the BIFF method for every communication: Brief, Informative, Friendly, Firm. Courts favor parents who communicate this way.
- Send communications through documented channels (email, text, or a co-parenting app) so you have a record.
- Do not add emotional language, sarcasm, or accusations. Your messages may be read aloud in court.
- Save a copy of every communication you send and receive.

## The Template

```
CO-PARENT COMMUNICATION TEMPLATES
Using the BIFF Method

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
THE BIFF METHOD
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Every communication with your co-parent should be:

  B — BRIEF:       Keep it short. 2-5 sentences maximum.
                    Say what needs to be said and stop.

  I — INFORMATIVE: Share facts, not feelings. Include dates,
                    times, and logistics. No opinions or
                    accusations.

  F — FRIENDLY:    Use a respectful, neutral tone. Start
                    with a courteous opening. This does not
                    mean you are friends — it means you are
                    demonstrating maturity to the court.

  F — FIRM:        State what you need clearly. End the
                    conversation. Do not leave room for
                    argument or back-and-forth debate.

Before sending any message, ask yourself:
  - Would I be comfortable with a judge reading this?
  - Does this focus on the child's needs?
  - Is this brief and factual?
  - Have I removed all emotional language?

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SCENARIO 1: DENIED VISITATION / PARENTING TIME
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Use when: You arrived for court-ordered parenting time and the
child was not produced, or the co-parent is refusing to allow
the scheduled exchange.

--- TEMPLATE ---

Subject: [Child's name] — Parenting Time [Date]

Hi [Co-parent's first name],

I was at [exchange location] at [time] for [Child's name]'s
scheduled parenting time per our court order dated [date of
order]. [Child's name] was not available for the exchange.

Please let me know when [Child's name] will be available for
make-up parenting time, as outlined in our order.

Thank you,
[Your name]

--- END TEMPLATE ---

Notes:
- Do not accuse. Do not ask "why."
- State the facts: where you were, when, what was supposed to
  happen, what did happen.
- Reference the court order by date.
- Request make-up time (shows you are child-focused).
- Screenshot and save immediately after sending.

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SCENARIO 2: SCHEDULE CHANGE REQUEST
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Use when: You need to request a change to the parenting
schedule, or the co-parent has requested one.

--- REQUESTING A CHANGE ---

Subject: [Child's name] — Schedule Change Request for [Date]

Hi [Co-parent's first name],

I am writing to request a schedule adjustment for [specific
date(s)]. [Brief reason — e.g., "I have a work obligation
on Saturday, March 15."]

I would like to propose [specific alternative — e.g.,
"exchanging on Friday evening instead of Saturday morning"
or "making up the time on [alternative date]"].

Please let me know if this works for you by [reasonable
deadline — e.g., "end of day Wednesday"].

Thank you,
[Your name]

--- RESPONDING TO A CHANGE REQUEST ---

Subject: Re: Schedule Change Request

Hi [Co-parent's first name],

Thank you for letting me know about [date]. [Choose one:]

[If agreeing:] That works for me. I will plan on [adjusted
arrangement]. Please confirm.

[If declining:] I am not able to accommodate that change.
I will plan on the regular schedule as outlined in our order.

[If proposing alternative:] I am not available on the proposed
date, but I could do [alternative]. Let me know.

Thank you,
[Your name]

--- END TEMPLATES ---

Notes:
- Be specific about dates and times.
- Offer a concrete alternative, not just "some other time."
- Set a deadline for a response.
- If the co-parent does not respond, follow the existing order.

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SCENARIO 3: MEDICAL DECISION NOTIFICATION
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Use when: You need to inform the co-parent about a medical
appointment, treatment, or decision, or you need to assert
your right to participate in medical decisions.

--- INFORMING CO-PARENT ---

Subject: [Child's name] — Medical Update

Hi [Co-parent's first name],

I am writing to let you know that [Child's name] has a
[type of appointment — e.g., "dental checkup" / "pediatrician
visit" / "specialist consultation"] scheduled for [date] at
[time] with [provider name] at [location].

[If applicable:] The doctor has recommended [brief description
of treatment or next steps]. I wanted to make sure you are
informed and have the opportunity to discuss this.

Please let me know if you have any questions or concerns.

Thank you,
[Your name]

--- ASSERTING YOUR RIGHT TO PARTICIPATE ---

Subject: [Child's name] — Medical Decision Participation

Hi [Co-parent's first name],

I understand that [Child's name] has [an upcoming appointment /
a medical decision pending / a new medication prescribed].
Per our custody order, I have the right to participate in
medical decisions for [Child's name].

Please provide me with the details of [the appointment /
the treatment plan / the provider] so that I can be informed
and involved.

Thank you,
[Your name]

--- END TEMPLATES ---

Notes:
- Always inform the co-parent of medical events during your
  parenting time.
- If you learn about a medical decision you were excluded from,
  document it and address it calmly through this template.
- Copy your attorney if medical exclusion is a pattern.

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SCENARIO 4: SCHOOL EVENT NOTIFICATION
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Use when: You need to inform the co-parent about a school
event, or you have been excluded from school communications
and need to assert your attendance.

--- INFORMING CO-PARENT ---

Subject: [Child's name] — School Event on [Date]

Hi [Co-parent's first name],

I wanted to let you know that [Child's name] has [event —
e.g., "a school concert" / "parent-teacher conference" /
"field day"] on [date] at [time] at [location].

I plan to attend. I wanted to make sure you are aware so
we can both be there to support [Child's name].

Thank you,
[Your name]

--- ASSERTING YOUR ATTENDANCE ---

Subject: [Child's name] — School Event Attendance

Hi [Co-parent's first name],

I have learned that [Child's name] has [event] on [date].
I plan to attend this event to support [Child's name], as
is my right under our custody arrangement.

I want [Child's name] to see both parents supporting [him/her/
them]. I will be respectful and focused on [Child's name].

Thank you,
[Your name]

--- END TEMPLATES ---

Notes:
- You have the right to attend your child's school events
  unless a court order specifically restricts you.
- Inform the school directly as well. Provide a copy of your
  custody order if needed.
- Keep all communication about school events child-focused.

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SCENARIO 5: HOLIDAY PLANNING
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Use when: You need to coordinate holiday parenting time,
or there is a dispute about holiday scheduling.

--- INITIATING HOLIDAY PLANNING ---

Subject: [Child's name] — [Holiday Name] Planning

Hi [Co-parent's first name],

I am writing to confirm plans for [Holiday — e.g.,
"Thanksgiving" / "Winter Break" / "Spring Break" / "Child's
birthday"] per our court order.

According to our order, [Child's name] is scheduled to be
with [me/you] from [date/time] to [date/time] this year.

I plan to [brief description of plans — e.g., "have dinner
with family" / "travel to visit grandparents in [state]"].
[If travel:] I will provide you with contact information and
an itinerary as required by our order.

Please confirm that this schedule works, or let me know if
you have questions, by [deadline].

Thank you,
[Your name]

--- RESPONDING TO A HOLIDAY DISPUTE ---

Subject: Re: [Holiday Name] Planning

Hi [Co-parent's first name],

I understand there is a question about the [Holiday]
schedule. Our court order dated [date] states [quote or
paraphrase the relevant provision].

Based on this, [Child's name] is scheduled to be with [me/you]
from [date/time] to [date/time]. I will follow the order
as written.

If you believe the order should be interpreted differently,
I suggest we [consult our attorneys / use our mediator /
address it through the court].

Thank you,
[Your name]

--- END TEMPLATES ---

Notes:
- Reference your court order by date and specific provision.
- Plan early. Send holiday communications at least 30 days
  in advance when possible.
- If the co-parent does not respond, follow the court order
  as written and document your attempts to communicate.
- Never argue about holidays in front of the child.
```

## Tips

- The BIFF method is not about being nice to someone who is harming your child. It is about protecting yourself in court. Every message you send can become an exhibit.
- Read every message out loud before sending it. If it sounds emotional, accusatory, or sarcastic, revise it.
- Do not respond to hostile messages with hostility. Respond with BIFF or do not respond at all (consult your attorney).
- Keep a record of all messages sent and received, including timestamps. A co-parenting app with built-in documentation is ideal.
- If the co-parent consistently fails to respond to reasonable communications, that pattern of non-responsiveness is itself evidence worth documenting.
