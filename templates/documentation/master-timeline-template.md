# Master Timeline Template

> **Audience:** Parents, Attorneys, Courts, GALs, Evaluators
> **Purpose:** Create a chronological, court-ready timeline of events that shows patterns of alienation, contact denial, and other relevant behaviors over time.
> **Related Skill:** paccs-tech-documentation

---

## How to Use This Template

- Enter events in strict chronological order, oldest first.
- Use factual, neutral language for every entry. Describe what happened, not how you felt about it.
- Assign a category to each event to make patterns visible at a glance.
- Reference your evidence for each entry so an attorney or judge can verify claims.
- Update the timeline regularly and review it with your attorney before court appearances.

## Tips

- A timeline that shows 20 incidents of access denial over six months is far more persuasive than describing one incident in detail. Patterns win cases.
- Include positive events (marked POS). This shows the court you are balanced and credible, not just compiling a grievance list.
- Cross-reference every timeline entry with your incident log for detailed supporting information.
- Print the timeline in table format for easy scanning by judges and attorneys. Courts process tables faster than narratives.
- Review your timeline with your attorney before every hearing. They may suggest adding or reordering entries for maximum impact.
