# Evidence Organization Checklist

> **Audience:** Parents, Attorneys
> **Purpose:** Provide a comprehensive checklist for collecting, organizing, and securely storing all types of evidence relevant to alienation and custody cases.
> **Related Skill:** paccs-tech-documentation

---

## How to Use This Template

- Go through each category below and check off what you have, what you need to collect, and where it is stored.
- Store all evidence in at least two locations (for example, a cloud drive and a physical binder at your attorney's office).
- Note the date each piece of evidence was obtained or created.
- Share this completed checklist with your attorney so they know what is available.
- Update this checklist regularly as new evidence is collected.

## Tips

- Collect evidence as it happens. Do not wait until you "need" it. By then, messages may be deleted and memories may fade.
- Preserve originals. Always keep the original file, screenshot, or document. Work from copies.
- Never alter evidence. Do not crop screenshots, edit messages, or rearrange content. Present everything as-is.
- Check recording laws in your state before recording any conversation. Your attorney can advise you.
- Include evidence of your positive parenting and cooperative behavior. This strengthens your credibility.
