# Communication Log Template

> **Audience:** Parents, Attorneys
> **Purpose:** Maintain a structured record of all co-parent communications to document cooperation, hostility, non-responsiveness, and patterns over time.
> **Related Skill:** paccs-tech-documentation

---

## How to Use This Template

- Log every communication with your co-parent, regardless of method (text, email, phone, in-person, through a third party).
- Complete each entry as soon as possible after the communication occurs.
- Use factual, neutral language in the summary column. Describe what was communicated, not how you felt about it.
- Save screenshots or other records of the communication and note where they are stored.
- Review the log regularly with your attorney to identify patterns (non-responsiveness, hostility, cooperation).

## Tips

- Courts value parents who communicate in a cooperative, child-focused manner. Your log will show your pattern of communication as well as your co-parent's.
- If your co-parent is non-responsive, the log documents that you attempted contact. This is important evidence.
- Consider using a co-parenting communication app that automatically timestamps and archives all messages. Some courts require these.
- Keep communication brief, informative, friendly, and firm (the BIFF method). See the Co-Parent Communication Templates for examples.
- Log communications even when nothing noteworthy happens. A consistent log is more credible than one with gaps.
