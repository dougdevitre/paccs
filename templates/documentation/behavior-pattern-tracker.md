# Behavior Pattern Tracker

> **Audience:** Parents, Attorneys, Evaluators, GALs
> **Purpose:** Track and categorize recurring alienating behaviors over time to reveal patterns that courts and evaluators need to see.
> **Related Skill:** paccs-tech-documentation, paccs-tech-advocate

---

## How to Use This Template

- Review the 10 alienating behavior categories below. For each one that applies to your situation, fill in the tracking table.
- Update this tracker weekly. Patterns become visible only when tracked consistently over time.
- Cross-reference each entry with your incident log for detailed documentation of specific events.
- Share the completed tracker with your attorney and any evaluator or GAL assigned to your case.
- Include positive observations as well. A balanced record is more credible.

## Tips

- Patterns matter more than individual incidents. A tracker showing "access denial" happening 12 times in three months is powerful evidence.
- Use the same category codes here and in your master timeline for easy cross-referencing.
- Note whether behaviors are stable, decreasing, or escalating. Escalation is a significant red flag for courts.
- Include the incident log entry numbers so anyone reviewing this tracker can find the full documentation for each example.
- Review this tracker with your attorney monthly. They can help you identify which patterns are most relevant to your legal strategy.
