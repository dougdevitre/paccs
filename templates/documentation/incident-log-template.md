# Incident Log Template

> **Audience:** Parents, Attorneys, Courts
> **Purpose:** Provide a structured, court-ready format for documenting individual incidents of alienation, contact denial, or other concerning events as they happen.
> **Related Skill:** paccs-tech-documentation, paccs-tech-advocate

---

## How to Use This Template

- Complete one entry for every incident, the same day it occurs. Same-day entries are more credible in court.
- Use factual, neutral language throughout. Describe only what you saw, heard, or received.
- Record the child's exact words in quotation marks. Do not paraphrase or interpret.
- Note all evidence collected and where it is stored (cloud, phone, attorney's office).
- Keep entries in chronological order in a dedicated binder, folder, or digital file.

---

## The Template

```
INCIDENT LOG ENTRY

Entry #: ______
Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
Time: [HH:MM AM/PM]
Location: [Where the incident happened — be specific]

People Present:
- [List every person who was there, including children]

What Happened:
[Factual description only. What you saw, heard, or received. No opinions, no
emotions, no interpretations. Write as though a stranger will read this and
needs to understand exactly what occurred.]

Child's Words (Verbatim):
"[Exact quote — use quotation marks. If the child did not speak, write 'N/A.'
Note who the child was speaking to and the context.]"

Child's Behavior:
[Observable actions only: cried, withdrew, clung to parent, repeated phrases,
refused to speak, appeared anxious, seemed rehearsed, etc. Do not diagnose.]

Evidence Collected:
- [ ] Screenshot — stored at: [location]
- [ ] Text message — stored at: [location]
- [ ] Email — stored at: [location]
- [ ] Photo — stored at: [location]
- [ ] Video — stored at: [location]
- [ ] Audio recording — stored at: [location] (check state recording laws)
- [ ] Witness statement — witness name: [name], contact: [info]
- [ ] Other: [describe] — stored at: [location]

Impact on Child:
[Observable behavior changes following the incident, if any. Examples: nightmares,
bedwetting, loss of appetite, clinginess, withdrawal, aggression, regressive
behaviors. Do not diagnose or attribute cause — just note what you observed.]

Action Taken:
[What you did in response. Examples: attempted to contact co-parent via text,
documented the incident, contacted attorney, filed a police report, contacted
the child's therapist.]

Reported To:
- [ ] Attorney — on [date]
- [ ] Guardian ad Litem (GAL) — on [date]
- [ ] Therapist — on [date]
- [ ] Police — report # [number] on [date]
- [ ] Child Protective Services — on [date]
- [ ] School — on [date]
- [ ] Other: [who] — on [date]

Follow-Up Needed:
- [ ] [Describe any follow-up actions required]

Entry Written By: [Your initials]
Date Written: [YYYY-MM-DD]
```

---

## Tips

- Write entries the same day. Courts give more weight to contemporaneous records than entries written days or weeks later.
- Stick to facts. "Co-parent did not arrive at the exchange location at the court-ordered time of 5:00 PM" is stronger than "Co-parent blew off the exchange again."
- Include positive interactions too. A log that only contains negative entries may appear one-sided to a judge.
- Back up every entry in at least two locations (for example, a cloud drive and your attorney's office).
- Review your log regularly with your attorney to ensure the format meets your jurisdiction's standards.
