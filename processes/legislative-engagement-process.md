# Legislative Engagement Process

> **Audience:** PACCS committees, advocates, volunteer organizers
> **Purpose:** End-to-end process for engaging legislators on child-centered safety reform
> **Related Skill:** paccs-tech-policy-education

---

## Overview

This document outlines the step-by-step process for identifying, contacting, meeting with, and maintaining relationships with legislators to advance child-centered safety legislation.

---

## Step 1: Research & Identify Targets

**Goal:** Identify the right legislators and understand their positions.

### Actions
- [ ] Identify your state's Judiciary Committee members (family law jurisdiction)
- [ ] Identify Children & Families Committee members
- [ ] Research each legislator's voting record on custody/family law bills
- [ ] Check if they have sponsored related legislation
- [ ] Identify their chief of staff and legislative aides (key contacts)
- [ ] Note their district — do you or other PACCS members live there? (constituent voices carry more weight)
- [ ] Check their public statements on family court, child welfare, or domestic violence

### Prioritization Matrix

| Priority | Criteria |
|----------|----------|
| **High** | Judiciary Committee member + constituent connection + no prior position |
| **Medium** | Relevant committee + open to meeting + no strong opposition |
| **Lower** | Not on relevant committee, but influential or sympathetic |

## Step 2: Initial Contact

**Goal:** Secure a meeting or establish a communication channel.

### Email Template

```
Subject: Meeting Request — Child-Centered Safety in [State] Family Courts

Dear [Legislator/Staff Name],

My name is [Name] and I am a [constituent of District X / representative of PACCS,
the Professional Alliance for Child Centered Safety].

I am writing to request a brief meeting to discuss [specific issue — e.g., the impact
of parental alienation on children in [State], proposed legislation on custody reform,
or gaps in family court protections].

PACCS is a nonpartisan organization focused on protecting children's emotional and
psychological safety. We provide evidence-based resources to families, courts, and
legislators.

I would welcome the opportunity to share research and practical recommendations.
I am available [dates/times] and happy to accommodate your schedule.

Thank you for your time and service.

Sincerely,
[Name]
[Contact Information]
[PACCS affiliation if applicable]
```

### Phone Script

```
"Hello, my name is [Name] and I'm calling to request a meeting with
[Legislator] regarding child-centered safety in family courts.

I'm a constituent in District [X] and I represent PACCS, the Professional
Alliance for Child Centered Safety. We have research and recommendations
on [specific topic] that we'd like to share.

Who would be the best person to schedule a meeting with?
Could I get their email to send a follow-up?"
```

### Tips
- Always lead with being a constituent if you are one
- Be specific about the topic — don't say "family law" generically
- Request a 15-20 minute meeting (easier to get than 30+)
- Follow up within 5 business days if no response
- Be persistent but respectful — staff are busy

## Step 3: Meeting Preparation

**Goal:** Arrive prepared, professional, and persuasive.

### Pre-Meeting Checklist

- [ ] Assemble briefing packet:
  - [ ] Legislative briefing one-pager (from `outreach/legislative/legislative-briefing-packet.md`)
  - [ ] Myth vs. Fact sheet relevant to the topic
  - [ ] Model statutory language if proposing legislation (from `outreach/legislative/model-statutory-language.md`)
  - [ ] Audience-specific advocacy asks (from `outreach/legislative/audience-specific-advocacy-asks.md`)
  - [ ] Research summary or fact sheet if applicable
- [ ] Prepare your personal connection (1-2 minutes, optional, anonymized)
- [ ] Identify your 3 key asks (specific, actionable, reasonable)
- [ ] Assign roles if attending as a group (lead speaker, note-taker, subject expert)
- [ ] Research the legislator's recent statements or votes on related topics
- [ ] Prepare for potential pushback (common objections and responses)
- [ ] Bring business cards or contact information

### Meeting Agenda (15-20 minutes)

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0-2 min | Introduction and thank you |
| 2-5 min | The problem (evidence-based, brief) |
| 5-8 min | Personal connection (optional, anonymized) |
| 8-12 min | Your specific asks (3 maximum) |
| 12-15 min | Questions and discussion |
| 15-17 min | Leave-behind materials and next steps |
| 17-20 min | Thank you and follow-up commitment |

## Step 4: The Meeting

**Goal:** Deliver your message clearly and secure commitments.

### Key Principles
- **Lead with the child** — frame everything around children's safety and wellbeing
- **Use data, not drama** — cite research, not emotional anecdotes
- **Be specific** — "We ask you to support HB 1234" is better than "We want change"
- **Listen** — understand their concerns, constraints, and priorities
- **Be nonpartisan** — PACCS is not a political organization
- **Leave materials** — always provide a physical leave-behind packet
- **Ask for a commitment** — even a small one ("Would you be willing to review this?")

### Common Objections & Responses

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "This is a family matter, not a legislative one" | "Children's psychological safety is a public interest. Courts need legislative guidance to protect children from documented harm." |
| "Both sides claim alienation" | "That's why evidence-based assessment standards are essential. Our model language requires professional evaluation, not parent claims." |
| "This could be used to silence abuse victims" | "Our framework requires safety screening. Genuine abuse concerns are always investigated. False allegations are themselves a form of abuse." |
| "We already have custody laws" | "Current laws lack specific provisions for alienation. Children fall through the gaps. [Cite specific state gap]." |

## Step 5: Follow-Up

**Goal:** Maintain the relationship and move toward action.

### Immediate Follow-Up (within 48 hours)
- [ ] Send thank-you email to the legislator and staff
- [ ] Attach any additional materials promised during the meeting
- [ ] Summarize agreed-upon next steps in writing
- [ ] Send to your PACCS team for documentation

### Thank-You Email Template

```
Subject: Thank You — [Topic] Meeting Follow-Up

Dear [Legislator/Staff Name],

Thank you for taking the time to meet with us on [date] to discuss
[topic]. We appreciate your willingness to listen and your thoughtful
questions.

As discussed, I am attaching [additional materials/research/model language].

We understood that [summarize any commitments or next steps agreed upon].

We are available to provide any additional information or support as you
consider this issue. Please don't hesitate to reach out.

Thank you for your service to the families of [State/District].

Sincerely,
[Name]
```

## Step 6: Bill Tracking & Testimony

**Goal:** Monitor legislation and provide support when needed.

### Bill Tracking Checklist
- [ ] Monitor state legislature website for relevant bills
- [ ] Set up alerts for keywords (parental alienation, custody, child safety, reunification)
- [ ] Track bill status (introduced, committee, floor vote, signed/vetoed)
- [ ] Classify bills as supportive, harmful, or dangerous using PACCS framework
- [ ] Prepare testimony when bills reach committee hearings (see `outreach/legislative/testimony-template.md`)
- [ ] Mobilize supporters for public comment periods
- [ ] Coordinate with allied organizations

### Legislation Classification

| Classification | Criteria | Action |
|---------------|----------|--------|
| **Supportive** | Recognizes alienation, protects children, requires training | Support publicly, offer testimony, provide materials |
| **Harmful** | Reduces protections, enables alienation, eliminates reunification | Oppose with evidence, provide counter-testimony, educate sponsors |
| **Dangerous** | Criminalizes alienation claims, mandates child-led severance | Urgent opposition, rapid-response alert, media outreach |
