# Material Distribution Workflow

A comprehensive workflow for getting PACCS educational and advocacy materials into the hands of decision-makers who can drive systemic change for child-centered safety.

---

## Overview

The most well-researched materials have zero impact if they never reach the people who make decisions. This workflow provides a systematic approach to identifying recipients, selecting appropriate materials, preparing distribution packages, delivering them through effective channels, and tracking results.

---

## Step 1: Identifying Target Recipients

### 1.1 Recipient Categories

| Category | Specific Targets | Why They Matter |
|----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Judiciary Committees** | State judiciary committees, Senate/House committees on children and families, joint committees on court reform | They shape court rules, judicial training requirements, and funding for family court |
| **Individual Legislators** | Sponsors of family law bills, committee chairs, legislators with personal connection to the issue | They introduce and vote on legislation affecting custody and child welfare |
| **Court Administrators** | Chief judges, court administrators, Administrative Office of the Courts | They set court policies, approve training programs, and allocate resources |
| **Professional Organizations** | State bar associations, psychological associations, social worker associations, judicial conferences | They set professional standards, offer continuing education, and influence members |
| **Child Welfare Agencies** | State child protective services directors, CASA/GAL program directors | They investigate allegations and make recommendations that affect families |
| **Educational Institutions** | Law school deans, social work program directors, psychology department chairs | They train the next generation of professionals who will handle these cases |
| **Media** | Family law reporters, investigative journalists, editorial boards, podcast hosts | They shape public awareness and create pressure for reform |

### 1.2 Recipient Research

For each target recipient, document:

- **Name and title**
- **Organization and role**
- **Mailing address** (office address, not personal)
- **Email address** (official)
- **Phone number** (office)
- **Staff contacts** (executive assistant, chief of staff, policy aide)
- **Known positions** on family court reform, child welfare, parental alienation
- **Previous interactions** with PACCS or allied organizations
- **Best approach** (formal letter, personal introduction, conference encounter)
- **Priority level** (Tier 1: critical, Tier 2: important, Tier 3: opportunity)

### 1.3 Prioritization Framework

**Tier 1 — Critical Targets** (personalized outreach required)
- Chairs of relevant committees
- Sponsors or co-sponsors of relevant legislation
- Chief judges in high-population jurisdictions
- Presidents of state bar and psychological associations

**Tier 2 — Important Targets** (standard outreach with personalization)
- Members of relevant committees
- Legislators with constituent interest in the issue
- Court administrators in key jurisdictions
- Professional organization education directors

**Tier 3 — Opportunity Targets** (standard outreach, bulk distribution)
- All members of relevant legislative bodies
- Conference attendees
- Professional organization members
- Media contacts

## Step 2: Material Selection

### 2.1 Matching Materials to Audience

| Audience | Primary Materials | Supporting Materials |
|----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Judges** | Judicial bench card, research summary, diagnostic code reference | Case law summary, reunification protocol overview |
| **Legislators** | Legislative one-pager, fiscal impact brief, constituent story (anonymized) | Research summary, model legislation |
| **Attorneys** | Practice guide, CLE opportunity notice, case strategy overview | Research citations, expert witness directory |
| **Mental Health Professionals** | Clinical overview, diagnostic criteria reference, treatment framework | CEU opportunity notice, assessment tools |
| **Court Administrators** | Training program proposal, cost-benefit analysis, implementation guide | Research summary, other jurisdiction examples |
| **Media** | Press kit, fact sheet, anonymized case examples, expert availability | Research summary, infographics, B-roll/photo assets |
| **General Public** | Myth vs. fact sheet, warning signs checklist, resource guide | Infographics, social media shareables |

### 2.2 Material Quality Checklist

Before any material goes out, verify:

- [ ] Current version (check date and version number)
- [ ] All citations accurate and current
- [ ] PACCS branding correctly applied
- [ ] Contact information current
- [ ] Disclaimer present
- [ ] No identifying information from real cases (unless consent obtained)
- [ ] Proofread for spelling, grammar, and formatting
- [ ] Print quality acceptable (if physical distribution)
- [ ] Digital version accessible (screen reader compatible, alt text on images)

## Step 3: Preparation

### 3.1 Printing Guidelines

| Material Type | Paper | Format | Notes |
|---------------|-------|--------|-------|
| Cover letter | Letterhead, 24 lb | Single page, one-sided | Personalized to each Tier 1 recipient |
| One-pagers | 28 lb, color | Single page, two-sided | Professional print quality |
| Briefing packets | 24 lb, color cover | Stapled or bound, 5-15 pages | Include table of contents |
| Fact sheets | 28 lb, color | Single page, two-sided | Lamination for conference handouts |
| Research summaries | 24 lb, B&W acceptable | Multi-page, stapled | Executive summary on first page |
| Business cards | Standard card stock | Standard size | Include QR code to PACCS resources |

### 3.2 Packaging Standards

**Tier 1 Packages** (high-priority individual recipients):
- Professional folder or presentation pocket
- Personalized cover letter
- 3-5 selected materials tailored to recipient
- Business card
- Follow-up meeting request (if applicable)

**Tier 2 Packages** (standard professional outreach):
- Professional folder
- Templated cover letter with personalized salutation
- 2-3 selected materials
- Business card

**Tier 3 Packages** (bulk distribution):
- Materials only (no folder required)
- General cover sheet
- Contact information card

### 3.3 Cover Letter Guidelines

Every distribution package should include a cover letter that:

- Addresses the recipient by name and title
- References the recipient's role or known interests
- States the purpose of the materials in one sentence
- Highlights 2-3 key takeaways the recipient should know
- Includes a specific ask (review, meeting, share with colleagues)
- Provides contact information for follow-up
- Is signed by an appropriate PACCS representative

## Step 4: Distribution Channels

### 4.1 In-Person Delivery

**Best for:** Tier 1 targets, legislative office visits, professional meetings

**Process:**
1. Request a meeting or appointment in advance
2. Confirm meeting details 48 hours before
3. Arrive 10 minutes early with materials organized
4. Deliver materials personally, walk through key points
5. Leave materials even if the meeting is with staff rather than the decision-maker
6. Record meeting notes within 24 hours

### 4.2 U.S. Mail

**Best for:** Tier 1 and Tier 2 targets, formal communications, jurisdictions not easily visited

**Process:**
1. Use professional packaging (manila envelope or flat mailer)
2. Include return address on the envelope
3. Use certified mail for Tier 1 targets (delivery confirmation)
4. Standard first-class mail for Tier 2 targets
5. Record tracking numbers and expected delivery dates

### 4.3 Email Distribution

**Best for:** Follow-ups, digital materials, newsletter distribution, Tier 3 targets

**Process:**
1. Use a professional email address (not personal)
2. Clear subject line: "PACCS — [Material Title] for [Recipient Role]"
3. Brief email body (3-5 sentences) with materials attached as PDF
4. Comply with CAN-SPAM requirements (unsubscribe option for bulk email)
5. Track open rates and click-through rates

### 4.4 Digital Platforms

**Best for:** Broad public awareness, ongoing education, resource sharing

**Channels:**
- PACCS website (resource library, download center)
- Social media (LinkedIn for professionals, Facebook for public awareness, X/Twitter for policy discussions)
- Professional platforms (state bar portals, association websites)
- Digital newsletters

### 4.5 Professional Conferences

**Best for:** Concentrated professional audiences, credibility building, networking

**Process:**
1. Identify relevant conferences 6-12 months in advance
2. Apply for exhibitor booth, presentation slot, or attendee registration
3. Prepare conference-specific materials (handouts, presentation slides, booth displays)
4. Staff booth or presentation with knowledgeable PACCS representatives
5. Collect contact information from interested attendees
6. Follow up within 1 week of conference

**Conference Types:**
- Judicial conferences and judicial education events
- State bar association annual meetings
- Psychological and social work association conferences
- Child welfare conferences
- Legislative days and advocacy events
- Family law CLE programs

## Step 5: Follow-Up Protocol

### 5.1 Follow-Up Timeline

| Distribution Method | First Follow-Up | Second Follow-Up | Final Follow-Up |
|---------------------|-----------------|------------------|-----------------|
| In-person delivery | Thank you within 24 hours | Check-in at 2 weeks | Quarterly update |
| U.S. Mail (Tier 1) | Confirm receipt at 1 week | Request meeting at 2 weeks | Monthly update |
| U.S. Mail (Tier 2) | Confirm receipt at 2 weeks | Check-in at 1 month | Quarterly update |
| Email | Follow-up at 1 week | Second attempt at 2 weeks | Move to newsletter list |
| Conference | Personal email within 1 week | Follow-up at 1 month | Add to regular outreach |

### 5.2 Follow-Up Methods

- **Phone call** — Best for Tier 1 targets, be prepared with a brief script
- **Email** — Best for general follow-ups, include a specific ask
- **Handwritten note** — High impact for Tier 1 targets after in-person meetings
- **Additional materials** — Send relevant new materials as they become available
- **Event invitations** — Invite contacts to PACCS webinars, events, or trainings

### 5.3 Follow-Up Tracking

For each follow-up, record:

- Date and method of follow-up
- Person contacted (or attempted)
- Response received (if any)
- Next action required
- Next follow-up date
- Notes on the interaction

## Step 6: Tracking and Reporting

### 6.1 CRM-Style Tracking

Maintain a centralized tracking system (spreadsheet, CRM, or database) with the following fields for each outreach effort:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Contact ID** | Unique identifier |
| **Name** | Full name |
| **Title** | Professional title |
| **Organization** | Employer or affiliation |
| **Category** | Judiciary, Legislature, Legal, Mental Health, etc. |
| **Tier** | 1, 2, or 3 |
| **State/Jurisdiction** | Geographic location |
| **Materials Sent** | List of materials with dates |
| **Distribution Method** | In-person, mail, email, conference, etc. |
| **Date Sent** | Date materials were distributed |
| **Follow-Up Dates** | Dates of each follow-up attempt |
| **Response** | No response, acknowledged, meeting requested, action taken |
| **Outcome** | Pending, positive, neutral, negative |
| **Next Action** | What to do next and when |
| **Notes** | Free-text field for additional context |

## Distribution Checklist

Use this checklist before each distribution batch:

### Pre-Distribution
- [ ] Target recipients identified and prioritized
- [ ] Materials selected and matched to audience
- [ ] All materials reviewed and current
- [ ] Cover letters drafted and personalized (Tier 1)
- [ ] Cover letters templated (Tier 2)
- [ ] Materials printed to quality standards
- [ ] Packages assembled per tier standards
- [ ] Return address and contact information verified
- [ ] Tracking spreadsheet set up with all recipients

### Distribution
- [ ] Packages mailed or delivered
- [ ] Tracking numbers recorded (if applicable)
- [ ] Digital materials sent via email
- [ ] Delivery confirmation obtained (Tier 1)
- [ ] Distribution log updated

### Post-Distribution
- [ ] Follow-up schedule created
- [ ] First follow-ups completed per timeline
- [ ] Responses logged in tracking system
- [ ] Non-responsive contacts flagged for second follow-up
- [ ] Positive responses escalated for deeper engagement
- [ ] Metrics compiled for reporting
