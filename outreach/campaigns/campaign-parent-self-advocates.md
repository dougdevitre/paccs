# Marketing Campaign: Empowering Parent Self-Advocates

> **Target Advocate Type:** Parents experiencing parental alienation, advocating for themselves and their children
> **Campaign Goal:** Equip parents with documentation tools, crisis resources, and professional connections to protect their children
> **Duration:** 8 weeks (ongoing nurture after)
> **Channels:** Facebook, Instagram, email, support groups, search/SEO

---

## Campaign Overview

A parent discovering they're being alienated from their child is often in crisis — confused, frightened, and isolated. They don't know the term "parental alienation." They don't know their rights. They don't know that documentation could save their case. They just know their child is slipping away.

This campaign meets parents where they are — in the search bar typing "my child won't see me," in the Facebook group asking "is this normal?" — and guides them from crisis to clarity with practical tools, professional connections, and a community that says "You are not crazy. This is real. Here's what to do."

---

## Target Audience Profile

**Who they are:**
- Parents (any gender) experiencing alienation, contact denial, or false allegations
- Ages 25-55, often mid-custody dispute or post-separation
- Searching online for answers, often late at night
- May or may not have an attorney yet
- Varying income levels — many facing financial strain from legal costs

**Pain points:**
- Isolation ("Nobody believes me")
- Overwhelm ("I don't know where to start")
- Fear ("I'm going to lose my child")
- Confusion ("Is this really happening?")
- Financial strain ("I can't afford another attorney")
- System failures ("The court doesn't care")
- Self-doubt ("Maybe it IS my fault")
- Urgency ("I have a hearing in 2 weeks")

---

## Key Messages

1. **"You are not crazy. What you're experiencing is real, it's documented, and it has a name."**
2. **"Document everything. If it's not written down, it didn't happen — in the eyes of the court."**
3. **"Your child needs you to survive this. Stay healthy. Stay ready."**
4. **"The right attorney, the right therapist, and the right documentation can change everything."**
5. **"You are not alone. Thousands of parents are fighting this same fight."**
6. **"Facts win cases. Emotions lose them. We'll help you build a factual record."**
7. **"This is a marathon, not a sprint. But every step you take matters."**

## Campaign Phases

### Phase 1: Awareness (Weeks 1-2)
**Theme:** "You Are Not Alone"
- Share validation content: "Signs you may be experiencing parental alienation"
- Run targeted ads to custody/divorce/family law search terms
- Post in parenting and divorce support communities
- Share the Victims Guide highlights
- Goal: Drive email sign-ups and resource downloads

### Phase 2: Engagement (Weeks 3-4)
**Theme:** "Here's What to Do"
- Release documentation templates (incident log, timeline, evidence checklist)
- Share the emergency action guide and safety plan
- Provide attorney vetting questions and co-parent scripts
- Host a webinar: "5 Steps to Protect Your Case"
- Goal: Template downloads and webinar attendance

### Phase 3: Activation (Weeks 5-6)
**Theme:** "Build Your Team"
- Guide parents to professional help (attorney, therapist, evaluator)
- Connect to local and online support groups
- Share court preparation resources
- Provide financial resource information (legal aid, pro bono)
- Goal: Professional connections and support group enrollment

### Phase 4: Sustained Action (Weeks 7-8+)
**Theme:** "Keep Going"
- Weekly encouragement and resource updates
- Community building (peer connections)
- Share success stories (anonymized)
- Ongoing documentation reminders and new tools
- Goal: Retention, community engagement, peer mentorship

What you're experiencing has a name: **parental alienation.** It's documented. It's researched. And most importantly — there are things you can do about it.

Here are your first 3 steps:

1. **Start documenting today.** Download our Incident Log Template — it's the format attorneys need. [link]
2. **Know your rights.** Our one-page Know Your Rights guide covers custody, school access, medical decisions, and more. [link]
3. **Create a safety plan.** If contact is being denied or you're facing false allegations, our Safety Plan Template walks you through it. [link]

You are not crazy. You are not alone. And your child needs you to keep going.

One entry today. Another tomorrow. That's how cases are built.

— The PACCS Team
