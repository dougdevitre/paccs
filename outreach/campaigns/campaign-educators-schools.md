# Marketing Campaign: Educating School Personnel on Parental Alienation

> **Target Advocate Type:** Teachers, school counselors, principals, school social workers
> **Campaign Goal:** Educate school personnel on alienation dynamics, parental rights (FERPA), and how to support children without taking sides
> **Duration:** 6 weeks
> **Channels:** Email, education conferences, teacher professional development, school newsletters

---

## Campaign Overview

Schools are often unknowing participants in parental alienation. When one parent controls the school relationship — enrolling the child without notifying the other parent, excluding them from conferences, or feeding a one-sided narrative to teachers — the school becomes a tool of the alienation.

Most educators have never heard of parental alienation. They default to the parent who shows up most, accept the child's stated preferences at face value, and may unknowingly reinforce the exclusion of a loving parent. This campaign changes that — equipping school personnel with the knowledge and tools to support every child's right to both safe parents.

---

## Target Audience Profile

**Who they are:**
- K-12 teachers, especially elementary and middle school
- School counselors and social workers
- Principals and assistant principals
- School registrars and front office staff
- Special education coordinators

**What motivates them:**
- Child wellbeing above all
- Following the law (FERPA, custody orders)
- Avoiding liability and conflict
- Creating a stable learning environment
- Being fair to all families

**Pain points:**
- Caught in the middle of custody disputes they don't understand
- Unsure of their legal obligations regarding both parents
- Receiving conflicting instructions from parents
- Worried about making the situation worse
- No training on alienation dynamics
- Pressure from one parent to exclude the other
- Children displaying behavioral issues they can't explain

---

## Key Messages

1. **"Every child has two parents — and both have legal rights to be involved in their education."**
2. **"Parental alienation affects children's learning, behavior, and emotional wellbeing at school."**
3. **"You don't need to take sides — you need to follow the law and support the child."**
4. **"FERPA requires both parents to have access to their child's educational records unless a court order says otherwise."**
5. **"When a child is caught in a loyalty conflict, school can be their one safe, neutral space."**
6. **"A parent being excluded from school events is a red flag — not a family preference to respect."**
7. **"You can make a difference by simply treating both parents equally."**

---

## Campaign Phases

### Phase 1: Awareness (Weeks 1-2)
- Share statistics on children affected by high-conflict custody
- Post about the hidden impact of alienation on learning
- Introduce PACCS as a resource for educators
- Highlight FERPA obligations in plain language

### Phase 2: Engagement (Weeks 3-4)
- Release the School Personnel Guide and FERPA checklist
- Share the school communication scripts
- Offer downloadable "Both Parents Matter" poster/handout for schools
- Host a webinar for school counselors

### Phase 3: Activation (Weeks 5-6)
- Provide professional development session outline for schools
- Connect interested educators with PACCS training resources
- Share case examples (anonymized) of how schools made a difference
- Encourage schools to adopt both-parent communication policies

### Phase 4: Sustained Action (Ongoing)
- Annual back-to-school awareness push
- Ongoing resource updates
- Partnership with education associations
- Recognition for schools implementing best practices

## Email Sequences

### Email 1: Introduction

**Subject:** What every educator should know about parental alienation

Dear Educator,

You may have a student in your classroom right now who is caught in something called parental alienation — where one parent is systematically turning the child against the other parent.

These children often show:
- Sudden behavioral changes or academic decline
- Anxiety around pickup/dropoff times
- Adult language when discussing a parent
- Refusal to acknowledge one parent exists

You don't need to diagnose it. You don't need to take sides. But you DO need to know it exists — because how you respond matters.

PACCS has free resources designed specifically for educators:

- **Glossary of Key Terms** — Understand the language
- **School Communication Scripts** — Know what to say in difficult situations
- **FERPA Quick Reference** — Your legal obligations to both parents

Download them here: [link]

Thank you for everything you do for children.

— The PACCS Team

### Email 2: Follow-Up (Week 2)

**Subject:** Are both parents getting your communications?

Hi [Name],

Quick question: Does your school automatically send report cards, event notices, and conference invitations to both parents?

If not, you may be unintentionally participating in one parent's exclusion — and potentially violating FERPA.

Here's what we recommend:
1. Ask your registrar: Are both parents listed for every student?
2. Default to sending all communications to both parents
3. Only restrict access when you have a court order on file
4. When in doubt, consult your school attorney

PACCS has a template letter that parents can use to assert their rights at your school. Being familiar with it helps you respond professionally: [link to resources/scripts/school/school-communication-scripts.md]

Small changes in your process can make a huge difference for a child.

— The PACCS Team
