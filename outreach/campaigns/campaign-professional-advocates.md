# Marketing Campaign: Equipping Professional Advocates

> **Target Advocate Type:** Professional advocates, case managers, victim services staff, nonprofit organizations
> **Campaign Goal:** Provide professional-grade tools, training resources, and evidence-based materials for working with alienated families
> **Duration:** 8 weeks
> **Channels:** LinkedIn, email, professional conferences, webinars

---

## Campaign Overview

Professional advocates — victim services staff, case managers, family support workers, and nonprofit leaders — encounter parental alienation regularly but rarely have specific training for it. They know high-conflict custody. They know domestic violence. But alienation occupies a unique space that most professional training programs don't address.

This campaign provides professional advocates with evidence-based tools, standardized frameworks, and a resource library that elevates their practice — helping them serve alienated families more effectively while maintaining professional boundaries and preventing burnout.

---

## Target Audience Profile

**Who they are:**
- Victim advocates and case managers in family court settings
- Nonprofit staff at organizations serving separated/divorced families
- Domestic violence advocates encountering custody-related alienation
- Family support workers and parent educators
- Advocacy organization leaders seeking resources for their teams

**Pain points:**
- Compassion fatigue from complex, long-running cases
- System failures they can't control (courts, CPS, biased professionals)
- Limited alienation-specific training (most training covers DV, not alienation)
- Ethical boundaries (wanting to help but not overstepping into legal/clinical advice)
- Resource scarcity (no standardized tools for alienation cases)
- Difficulty distinguishing alienation from estrangement or legitimate safety concerns
- Organizational pressure to serve more clients with fewer resources

---

## Key Messages

1. **"Parental alienation is a form of child abuse — and your training should reflect that."**
2. **"Evidence-based tools make you more effective and protect your professional boundaries."**

5. **"Understanding alienation dynamics protects you from being manipulated by alienating parents."**
6. **"Your professional development is an investment in the families you serve."**
7. **"PACCS provides the tools. You provide the human connection."**

## Campaign Phases

### Phase 1: Awareness (Weeks 1-2)
**Theme:** "Alienation Is in Your Caseload — Do You Recognize It?"
- Share statistics on alienation prevalence in family court
- Post case pattern examples (anonymized)
- Highlight the gap between need and training
- Introduce PACCS as a professional resource partner

### Phase 2: Engagement (Weeks 3-4)
**Theme:** "Professional Tools for Professional Advocates"
- Release the PACCS template and script library for advocate use
- Share the alienation vs. estrangement chart (assessment tool)
- Provide myth-vs-fact sheets for professional distribution
- Host a webinar: "Recognizing and Responding to Alienation in Your Practice"

### Phase 3: Activation (Weeks 5-6)
**Theme:** "Integrate, Train, Lead"
- Provide training module outlines for organizational use
- Connect advocates with PACCS committee resources
- Offer organizational partnership framework
- Share process documents for campaign and outreach work

### Phase 4: Sustained Action (Weeks 7-8+)
**Theme:** "A Professional Community"
- Quarterly resource updates
- Peer learning opportunities
- Case consultation frameworks (anonymized)
- Annual professional development events
