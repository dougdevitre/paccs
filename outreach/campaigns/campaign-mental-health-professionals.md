# Marketing Campaign: Engaging Mental Health Professionals

> **Target Advocate Type:** Mental health professionals (therapists, psychologists, counselors, reunification specialists)
> **Campaign Goal:** CEU training and clinical assessment tools for identifying and addressing parental alienation
> **Duration:** 8 weeks
> **Channels:** LinkedIn, Psychology Today, CEU platforms, conferences

---

## Campaign Overview

Mental health professionals are among the most critical — and most under-equipped — stakeholders in parental alienation cases. Graduate programs rarely address coercive control dynamics in post-separation families, leaving clinicians without the diagnostic frameworks, assessment tools, or ethical guidance needed to distinguish alienation from estrangement. The result is a treatment gap where children's psychological harm goes unrecognized, and clinicians may inadvertently reinforce the alienating dynamic by deferring to a child's stated preferences without assessing for manipulation.

This campaign provides mental health professionals with peer-reviewed research summaries, validated clinical assessment frameworks, and CEU training opportunities that bridge the gap between current practice and the growing evidence base on parental alienation. By meeting clinicians where they already seek professional development — LinkedIn, Psychology Today profiles, CEU platforms, and professional conferences — this campaign positions PACCS as a credible, research-grounded resource for ethical clinical practice in high-conflict custody cases.

---

## Target Audience Profile

**Who they are:**
- Licensed therapists (LMFT, LCSW, LPC) working with children and families
- Psychologists conducting custody evaluations or court-ordered assessments
- School counselors encountering alienation dynamics in student behavior
- Reunification therapists and specialists in court-ordered family therapy
- Play therapists and child development specialists
- Clinicians seeking CEU credits in family systems or child welfare

**What motivates them:**
- Ethical obligation to assess accurately and avoid harm
- Professional development and evidence-based practice
- Desire to serve children's best interests, not parental agendas
- Staying current with evolving research on coercive control and family dynamics
- CEU requirements and licensure maintenance

**Pain points:**
- **Ethical dilemmas in competing narratives:** Clinicians receive conflicting accounts from parents and struggle to determine which narrative reflects reality without validated assessment tools
- **Lack of graduate-level alienation training:** Most clinical programs do not address alienation, leaving practitioners reliant on incomplete frameworks or outdated assumptions
- **Risk of reinforcing alienation dynamics:** Without specialized knowledge, a clinician may validate the alienating parent's narrative, restrict the targeted parent's access, or pathologize the targeted parent's distress
- **Child's stated preference vs. manipulation:** Clinicians face pressure to honor a child's expressed wishes without the tools to assess whether those wishes reflect autonomous preference or coerced alignment
- **Fear of professional backlash:** Raising alienation in clinical settings can invite accusations of bias, gender discrimination, or minimizing abuse, creating professional risk
- **Court system pressures:** Clinicians ordered into high-conflict cases may lack training in forensic contexts, appropriate documentation, or understanding of how their reports influence custody outcomes
- **Diagnostic confusion:** Alienation symptomatology overlaps with anxiety, depression, and adjustment disorders, leading to misdiagnosis and misdirected treatment

## Campaign Phases

### Phase 1: Clinical Awareness (Weeks 1-2)
**Theme:** "What the Research Actually Shows"

- Publish research summary briefs on LinkedIn and Psychology Today
- Share peer-reviewed findings on alienation as psychological maltreatment
- Distribute the alienation-vs-estrangement differential assessment chart
- Highlight gaps in graduate-level training with survey data from practicing clinicians

### Phase 2: Clinical Education (Weeks 3-4)
**Theme:** "Assessment Tools for Ethical Practice"

- Release clinical assessment frameworks and screening indicators
- Share case vignettes illustrating alienation vs. estrangement presentations
- Distribute the reunification therapist vetting guide as a peer-review standard
- Offer downloadable clinical checklists for intake and ongoing assessment

### Phase 3: Professional Development (Weeks 5-6)
**Theme:** "CEU Training That Closes the Gap"

- Announce CEU-eligible webinar series on alienation assessment and treatment
- Partner with CEU platforms for accredited course offerings
- Share conference presentation abstracts and speaking opportunities
- Facilitate peer consultation group sign-ups for ongoing case support

### Phase 4: Sustained Clinical Integration (Weeks 7-8)
**Theme:** "Integrating Evidence into Practice"

- Highlight clinicians who have integrated alienation screening into practice
- Release case outcome data from reunification programs using evidence-based models
- Provide template language for clinical reports addressing alienation findings
- Establish ongoing peer consultation network for complex cases
