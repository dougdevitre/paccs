# Marketing Campaign: Continuing Education and Courtroom Resources for Legal Professionals

> **Target Audience:** Legal professionals (family law attorneys, judges, guardians ad litem, custody evaluators)
> **Campaign Goal:** Provide CLE content and courtroom resources grounded in peer-reviewed research on parental alienation dynamics
> **Duration:** 8 weeks
> **Channels:** LinkedIn, CLE events, bar associations

---

## Campaign Overview

Parental alienation cases present some of the most complex fact patterns in family law. Professionals tasked with adjudicating or evaluating these cases frequently report insufficient training, inconsistent standards across jurisdictions, and difficulty distinguishing alienation from estrangement rooted in legitimate safety concerns. The result is a system where outcomes depend heavily on which professionals are involved rather than on the quality of the evidence presented.

This campaign delivers peer-reviewed research, structured assessment frameworks, and practical courtroom tools to legal professionals through channels they already trust. By partnering with bar associations and CLE providers, PACCS positions itself as a credible, research-first resource rather than an advocacy organization, meeting professionals where they are with materials that meet the evidentiary and ethical standards they require.

---

## Target Audience Profile

**Who they are:**
- Family law attorneys representing parents in custody disputes
- Family court judges and magistrates
- Guardians ad litem (GALs) appointed to represent children's interests
- Custody evaluators (psychologists, social workers conducting court-ordered evaluations)
- Mediators and parenting coordinators in high-conflict cases

**What motivates them:**
- Obligation to make decisions that protect children's wellbeing
- Professional duty to apply current research to practice
- Need for defensible, evidence-based reasoning in orders and recommendations
- CLE credit requirements for license maintenance

**Pain points:**
- Alienation cases involve overlapping behavioral indicators that are difficult to parse without specialized training
- Limited formal education on alienation dynamics in law school or professional certification programs
- No universally adopted assessment standard or diagnostic framework across jurisdictions
- Risk of reaching incorrect conclusions when alienation is mistaken for estrangement (or vice versa)
- Exposure to professional liability when decisions lack grounding in current literature
- High-conflict parties who present competing and contradictory narratives with equal conviction
- Time constraints that make deep research into alienation literature impractical during active cases

## Campaign Phases

### Phase 1: Credibility Establishment (Weeks 1-2)
**Theme:** "Bridging the Research-Practice Gap"

- Publish LinkedIn articles summarizing key findings from peer-reviewed alienation research (Bernet, Harman, Lorandos)
- Distribute the judicial bench card to family court contacts as a no-cost professional resource
- Submit CLE program proposals to state and local bar associations
- Share data on the training gap: percentage of family law professionals reporting no formal alienation education

### Phase 2: Resource Distribution (Weeks 3-4)
**Theme:** "Tools for the Courtroom and the Evaluation Office"

- Launch email sequence delivering assessment tools, the alienation-vs-estrangement differential chart, and the false allegations fact sheet
- Promote downloadable resources through LinkedIn with direct links
- Publish case-study summaries (anonymized, jurisdiction-neutral) illustrating correct and incorrect identification
- Partner with at least two bar association publications for guest articles

### Phase 3: CLE Engagement (Weeks 5-6)
**Theme:** "Earn Credit While Closing the Knowledge Gap"

- Host or co-host a live CLE webinar: "Alienation Dynamics in Custody Litigation: What the Research Shows"
- Offer on-demand CLE module for professionals unable to attend live sessions
- Distribute pre- and post-session knowledge assessments to document learning outcomes
- Collect participant feedback and testimonials for future promotion

### Phase 4: Sustained Professional Network (Weeks 7-8)
**Theme:** "Building an Interdisciplinary Community of Practice"

- Invite CLE participants to a quarterly professional roundtable series
- Release model statutory language resource with implementation guidance
- Publish a summary report of campaign reach, CLE completion rates, and knowledge assessment results
- Establish referral network connecting trained attorneys, evaluators, and GALs
