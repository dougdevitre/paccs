# Video Script: What Is Parental Alienation?

**PACCS — Professional Alliance for Child Centered Safety**
**Audience:** General Public and Professionals
**Format:** 60-Second Explainer Video Script
**Estimated Runtime:** 55-60 seconds

---

## Script

| Timecode | Visual Direction | Narration | On-Screen Text |
|----------|-----------------|-----------|----------------|
| 0:00-0:05 | Warm, neutral background. PACCS logo fades in. | — | **"What Is Parental Alienation?"** PACCS logo |
| 0:05-0:15 | Simple animation: two parent figures on either side of a child figure, connected by lines representing relationships. | "Parental alienation happens when one parent turns a child against the other parent — through put-downs, interference with contact, and manipulation." | **Parental alienation** = one parent undermining a child's relationship with the other parent |
| 0:15-0:25 | Animation: one line between parent and child breaks. The child figure moves away from one parent. | "Over time, the child may reject the targeted parent entirely — not because of anything that parent did, but because of the alienating parent's behavior." | **The child rejects a loving parent** — not from experience, but from influence |
| 0:25-0:35 | Data visualization: bar graph or infographic showing key statistics. | "Research published in peer-reviewed journals shows that about 1 in 100 children experience alienation, and more than 1 in 8 parents report alienating behaviors from a co-parent." | **~1 in 100 children affected** | **1 in 8 parents report alienating behaviors** | Sources: Harman et al., 2022; Harman et al., 2019 |
| 0:35-0:45 | Icons representing anxiety, depression, broken heart, representing documented harms. | "Children who are alienated from a parent experience real, documented harm — including anxiety, depression, and long-term difficulties with relationships and trust." | **Documented harm to children:** anxiety, depression, relationship difficulties |
| 0:45-0:55 | Animation: child figure reconnected with both parent figures. PACCS Five Pillars briefly shown as icons. | "Parental alienation is a child protection issue. Every child deserves the safety of loving both parents without fear." | **This is a child protection issue.** |
| 0:55-1:00 | PACCS logo, website URL, call to action. | "Learn more at [PACCS website]." | **Learn more: [PACCS website URL]** | **#ParentalAlienation #ChildCenteredSafety** |

## Production Notes

- **Tone:** Calm, informative, empathetic. Not alarmist.
- **Narrator voice:** Professional, warm, gender-neutral delivery.
- **Music:** Soft, neutral background music. No dramatic or emotionally manipulative scoring.
- **Color palette:** Use PACCS brand colors. Warm, accessible tones.
- **Accessibility:** Include closed captions. Ensure on-screen text meets contrast standards. Provide audio description for visuals.
