## Production Notes

- **Tone:** Clear, factual, non-alarmist. Present warning signs as indicators, not diagnoses.
- **Narrator voice:** Professional, measured, authoritative but approachable.
- **Music:** Subtle background score. Neutral and steady.
- **Pacing:** Each sign gets approximately 8 seconds. Keep text on screen long enough to read.
- **Accessibility:** Closed captions required. High-contrast on-screen text. Audio description for visuals.

---

## Call to Action Options

- "If you recognize these signs, consult a mental health professional with alienation training."
- "Share this video to help teachers, counselors, and family members recognize alienation."
- "Learn more at [PACCS website URL]."

## Key Research References

These warning signs are drawn from the clinical literature on parental alienation, including:

- Baker, A.J.L., & Darnall, D. (2006). Behaviors and strategies employed in parental alienation.
- Gardner, R.A. (1998). Eight manifestations of parental alienation (as updated and refined by subsequent researchers).
- Bernet, W. (2010). *Parental Alienation, DSM-5, and ICD-11.*
- Warshak, R.A. (2015). Ten parental alienation fallacies that compromise decisions in court and in therapy.
