## Tips for Press Releases

- **Keep it to one page** (approximately 400-500 words).
- **Use AP style** for dates, numbers, and titles.
- **Include hyperlinks** to research, reports, or resources referenced.
- **Distribute via** email to media contacts, wire services, and post on the PACCS website.
- **Embargo:** If the release is embargoed, clearly state the embargo date and time at the top.
- **Follow up** with journalists 2-3 days after distribution.

**PACCS Publishes Comprehensive Research Summary on Parental Alienation and Child Outcomes**

*New resource synthesizes three decades of peer-reviewed research for policymakers and the public*

**[CITY, STATE]** — The Professional Alliance for Child Centered Safety (PACCS) today released a comprehensive research summary documenting the impact of parental alienation on children's psychological health and long-term well-being. The summary synthesizes findings from more than 100 peer-reviewed studies spanning three decades of clinical research.

The research summary documents that children subjected to parental alienation experience significantly elevated rates of anxiety, depression, low self-esteem, and substance abuse. Studies further indicate that the effects persist into adulthood, affecting the ability to form and maintain healthy relationships.

"Children who are systematically prevented from having a relationship with a loving, fit parent suffer real and documented psychological harm," said [Name], [Title] of PACCS. "This research summary makes the evidence accessible to legislators, judges, and the public so that policy decisions can be grounded in science rather than ideology."

The summary is organized by topic area — including child outcomes, assessment methods, intervention effectiveness, and legal research — and is available for download at no cost on the PACCS website.

**About PACCS**

The Professional Alliance for Child Centered Safety (PACCS) is a multidisciplinary organization dedicated to protecting children's psychological safety and their right to healthy relationships with both parents. For more information, visit [website URL].

**PACCS Announces Support for [State] Child-Centered Safety Act**

*Proposed legislation would define parental alienation and mandate training for family court professionals*

**[CITY, STATE]** — The Professional Alliance for Child Centered Safety (PACCS) today announced its support for [Bill Number], the [State] Child-Centered Safety Act, introduced by [Representative/Senator Name]. The bill would establish an evidence-based statutory definition of parental alienation and require family court judges and custody evaluators to complete specialized training on alienation dynamics.

Research estimates that parental alienation affects approximately 1% of all children and a substantially higher percentage of children in separated and divorced families. Despite the documented impact on children's mental health, [State] currently has no statutory framework for identifying or addressing alienation in family court.

"This legislation represents a critical step toward ensuring that family courts in [State] can identify and respond to parental alienation based on the best available evidence," said [Name], [Title] of PACCS. "Every child deserves the protection of a legal system that understands the psychological harm of being turned against a loving parent."

PACCS urges residents of [State] to contact their legislators in support of [Bill Number]. Additional information, including a legislative briefing packet, is available at [website URL].
