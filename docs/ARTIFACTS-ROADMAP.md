# PACCS Artifact Roadmap for Parental Alienation Advocates

A prioritized list of resources, tools, and materials to build for supporting victims of parental alienation. Organized by category and urgency.

---

## Tier 1: Immediate Need (Build First)

### For Targeted Parents
- [ ] **Alienation Identification Checklist** — printable checklist of alienating behaviors with space to document dates and examples
- [ ] **Incident Documentation Template** — court-ready log format for recording alienating behaviors as they happen
- [ ] **Emergency Action Guide** — what to do in the first 24 hours when contact is denied or false allegations are made
- [ ] **One-Page Situation Summary Template** — structured format for presenting your case to attorneys, GALs, and judges
- [ ] **Evidence Organization Checklist** — how to collect, organize, and preserve texts, emails, recordings, and witness statements
- [ ] **Safety Plan Template** — personal safety plan including emergency contacts, documentation systems, and support networks
- [ ] **Know Your Rights One-Pager** — parental rights in custody, school access, medical decisions, and communication (state-specific versions)
- [ ] **Attorney Vetting Questionnaire** — questions to assess whether a family law attorney understands parental alienation
- [ ] **Co-Parent Communication Templates** — neutral, documented, boundary-maintaining scripts for high-conflict situations
- [ ] **Self-Care Guide for Targeted Parents** — resilience strategies for the emotional toll of alienation

### For Children & Reunification
- [ ] **Reunification Readiness Checklist** — self-assessment for parents preparing for contact restoration
- [ ] **Age-Appropriate Letter Templates** — reconnection letters for children at each developmental stage
- [ ] **Reunification Therapist Vetting Guide** — questions and red flags when selecting a reunification professional
- [ ] **Understanding Your Child's Behavior Guide** — helping parents interpret rejection without taking it personally

### For Courts & Legal Professionals
- [ ] **Judicial Bench Card: Parental Alienation** — one-page quick reference for judges (warning signs, risk factors, evidence considerations)
- [ ] **False Allegations Fact Sheet** — research-backed information on prevalence, patterns, and identification
- [ ] **Alienation vs. Estrangement Comparison Chart** — clear distinctions for legal and clinical professionals
- [ ] **Court-Ready Documentation Guide** — what courts look for in alienation evidence

## How to Use This Roadmap

1. **Start with Tier 1** — these address the most urgent needs of families in crisis
2. **Build Tier 2 in parallel** — educational foundations support everything else
3. **Tier 3 creates systemic change** — advocacy and outreach multiply the impact
4. **Tier 4 raises the professional standard** — training creates lasting reform
5. **Tier 5 builds sustainability** — long-term infrastructure for the organization

### For Each Artifact, Define:
- **Owner** (which committee or member leads)
- **Collaborators** (which committees contribute)
- **Audience** (who uses this)
- **Format** (PDF / video / web / training module)
- **Deadline** (when it must be complete)
- **Review process** (Research Integrity + Lived Experience review)
