# Reunification Readiness Checklist

> **Audience:** Parents, Therapists
> **Purpose:** Provide a comprehensive self-assessment for parents preparing for reunification with their child after a period of alienation or separation, covering emotional, practical, professional, communication, and self-care readiness.
> **Related Skill:** paccs-tech-reunification

---

## How to Use This Template

- Complete this checklist honestly. It is a self-assessment, not a test. There are no right or wrong answers.
- Review each section and check the items you have addressed. Identify areas that need more work before reunification begins.
- Share this completed checklist with your reunification therapist and attorney so they can help you address gaps.
- Revisit this checklist monthly as you prepare for and move through the reunification process.
- Remember that reunification is a process, not an event. Being "ready" does not mean being perfect. It means being prepared.

## Tips

- You do not need a perfect score to begin reunification. The purpose of this checklist is to identify gaps so you can address them, not to create a barrier to seeing your child.
- Share your results honestly with your therapist. They can help you strengthen weak areas and give you confidence in your strong areas.
- The emotional readiness section is the most important. If you are still overwhelmed by grief or anger, work with your therapist before the first contact so those emotions do not spill onto your child.
- Prepare for the long haul. Reunification with an alienated child is one of the hardest things a parent can do. It is also one of the most important.
- Celebrate small wins. A one-word response from your child, a brief moment of eye contact, or a small laugh during a visit — these are milestones worth recognizing.
