# Safety Plan Template

> **Audience:** Parents, Advocates
> **Purpose:** Create a comprehensive personal safety plan for parents facing alienation, contact denial, false allegations, or coercive control, so they are prepared to act quickly and effectively when a crisis occurs.
> **Related Skill:** paccs-tech-crisis-safety

---

## How to Use This Template

- Fill in every section thoroughly. A safety plan is only useful if it is complete before a crisis happens.
- Review and update this plan every 30 days, or immediately after any significant change in your situation.
- Store copies in multiple secure locations (your home, your attorney's office, with a trusted person).
- Share relevant sections with your support team so they know their role if a crisis occurs.
- Practice your plan mentally. In a crisis, you will fall back on what you have rehearsed.

## Tips

- Complete this plan when you are calm, not during a crisis. You will not be able to think clearly under pressure, so do the thinking now.
- Review this plan with your attorney and therapist. They may suggest additions specific to your situation and jurisdiction.
- Keep a printed copy in your car or bag for emergencies, but redact sensitive information (names, phone numbers) from any copy that could be accessed by the co-parent.
- Practice the "If contact is denied" and "If false allegations are made" sections mentally. Knowing your first three steps can prevent costly mistakes.
- Update the review date and actually review it. Situations change, and your plan needs to change with them.
