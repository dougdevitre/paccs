# Emergency Action Guide: First 24 Hours

> **Audience:** Parents
> **Purpose:** Provide clear, step-by-step action plans for the first 24 hours of three common crisis scenarios: contact denied, false allegations made, and child in danger.
> **Related Skill:** paccs-tech-crisis-safety

---

## How to Use This Template

- Read through all three scenarios now, before a crisis happens. Familiarity reduces panic.
- When a crisis occurs, go directly to the relevant scenario and follow the steps in order.
- Check off each step as you complete it. Do not skip steps.
- Contact your attorney as early in the process as possible.
- After the first 24 hours, transition to your full Safety Plan for ongoing guidance.

  [ ] 6. CONTACT YOUR ATTORNEY:

         Report #: [___________]

  [ ] 8. NOTIFY YOUR SUPPORT TEAM:
         [ ] Trusted person: [name] at [time]
         [ ] Therapist: [name] at [time]
         [ ] GAL (if assigned): [name] at [time]

HOUR 4-24: DOCUMENTATION AND NEXT STEPS

  [ ] 9. WRITE A FULL INCIDENT LOG ENTRY while details are fresh.

  [ ] 10. CONTINUE FOLLOWING ALL OTHER COURT ORDERS.
          Do not retaliate, withhold support, or violate any
          order yourself.

  [ ] 11. SEND A FOLLOW-UP written communication (text or email)
          to co-parent:
          "[Child's name]'s court-ordered parenting time was
          scheduled for [date/time]. I was at [location] and
          [child] was not produced. Please confirm when [child]
          will be available for the next scheduled parenting time."

  [ ] 12. DISCUSS WITH ATTORNEY:
          [ ] Filing for contempt of court
          [ ] Emergency motion for enforcement
          [ ] Modifying the custody order

DO NOT:
  - Break into the other parent's home
  - Confront the co-parent aggressively
  - Make threats via text, email, or voicemail
  - Involve the child in the adult conflict
  - Stop following existing court orders yourself
  - Post about the situation on social media

SCENARIO 2: FALSE ALLEGATIONS MADE AGAINST YOU
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Use this when: You have been accused of abuse, neglect, or
endangerment. CPS has contacted you, police are investigating,
or the co-parent has made allegations to the court.

HOUR 0-1: IMMEDIATE ACTIONS

  [ ] 1. DO NOT PANIC. False allegations are a known alienation
         tactic. How you respond matters enormously.

  [ ] 2. DO NOT SPEAK TO INVESTIGATORS WITHOUT YOUR ATTORNEY.
         Say: "I want to cooperate fully. I would like my
         attorney present. Here is my attorney's contact
         information."
         (This is your right. It is not suspicious.)

  [ ] 3. DO NOT CONTACT THE ACCUSING PARTY directly.
         No calls, texts, emails, or in-person contact.

  [ ] 4. CONTACT YOUR ATTORNEY IMMEDIATELY:
         Called at [time]: [ ] Reached  [ ] Left message
         If you do not have an attorney:
         - Legal aid: lawhelp.org
         - ABA Free Legal Answers: abafreelegalanswers.org
         - Court self-help center: [local courthouse website]

HOUR 1-4: EVIDENCE PRESERVATION

  [ ] 5. PRESERVE ALL EVIDENCE — do not delete anything:
         [ ] Text messages with co-parent (screenshot + backup)
         [ ] Emails with co-parent
         [ ] Social media posts by co-parent
         [ ] Voicemails
         [ ] Any communications before/after the allegation

  [ ] 6. WRITE DOWN YOUR ACCOUNT OF EVENTS immediately:
         Date of alleged incident: [___________]
         Time: [___________]
         Where I actually was: [___________]
         Who was with me: [___________]
         Evidence of my whereabouts:
           [ ] Receipts (store, gas, restaurant)
           [ ] GPS/location data (phone, car)
           [ ] Security camera footage
           [ ] Witness: [Name: _________ Phone: _________]
           [ ] Witness: [Name: _________ Phone: _________]
           [ ] Work records / login records
           [ ] Other: [___________]

  [ ] 7. GATHER PRIOR FALSE ALLEGATION HISTORY (if applicable):
         Previous allegation #1: [date, type, outcome]
         Previous allegation #2: [date, type, outcome]
         Previous allegation #3: [date, type, outcome]

HOUR 4-24: RESPONSE AND PLANNING

  [ ] 8. REQUEST COPIES of any reports filed against you.
         Agency: [___________]
         Request made at [time] on [date]

  [ ] 9. IDENTIFY CHARACTER WITNESSES:
         [ ] Teachers/coaches: [names]
         [ ] Neighbors: [names]
         [ ] Family members: [names]
         [ ] Co-workers: [names]
         [ ] Other: [names]

  [ ] 10. GATHER EVIDENCE OF POSITIVE PARENTING:
          [ ] Photos/videos of you with the child
          [ ] School involvement records
          [ ] Medical appointment attendance records
          [ ] Extracurricular involvement records
          [ ] Cards, letters, gifts from/to the child

  [ ] 11. CONTINUE FOLLOWING ALL COURT ORDERS EXACTLY.
          Do not change your behavior. Do not avoid the child.
          Do not give the appearance of guilt.

  [ ] 12. DISCUSS WITH ATTORNEY:
          [ ] Response strategy
          [ ] Whether to request a polygraph (attorney's call)
          [ ] Protective motions
          [ ] Counter-evidence presentation

DO NOT:
  - Talk to investigators without your attorney
  - Contact the accusing party
  - Delete any messages, posts, or records
  - Discuss the allegations on social media
  - Stop exercising your parenting time
  - Assume it will "just go away"

  [ ] 5. DOCUMENT EVERYTHING YOU KNOW ABOUT THE DANGER:

         Who told me (if applicable): [________________________________]

  [ ] 7. WORK WITH ATTORNEY ON EMERGENCY LEGAL OPTIONS:

         [ ] Child's therapist: [name] at [time]

  [ ] 10. DO NOT ATTEMPT TO REMOVE THE CHILD IN VIOLATION
          OF A COURT ORDER. Go through emergency legal channels.

DO NOT:

  - Wait and see if it gets better
  - Assume someone else will handle it
  - Post about it on social media

## Tips

- Read all three scenarios before a crisis happens. You will not have time to read carefully when you are panicking.
- The single most important step in every scenario is to contact your attorney. If you do nothing else, do that.
- Everything you say and do during a crisis can and will be used in court. Stay calm, stay factual, stay documented.
- After the first 24 hours, review your full Safety Plan and update it based on what happened.
- Take care of yourself. Call your therapist or support person. You cannot help your child if you are falling apart.
