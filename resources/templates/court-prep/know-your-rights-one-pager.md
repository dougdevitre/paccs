# Know Your Rights: One-Pager for Parents

> **Audience:** Parents
> **Purpose:** Provide a quick-reference guide to the most common parental rights so parents can understand what they are entitled to and recognize when those rights are being violated.
> **Related Skill:** paccs-tech-advocate

---

## How to Use This Template

- Read through the rights categories below to understand the general landscape of parental rights in the United States.
- Check the boxes next to any rights you believe are being violated in your situation.
- Research your specific state's laws or ask your attorney to confirm which rights apply in your jurisdiction. Rights vary by state.
- Bring this document to your attorney consultation to discuss which rights are relevant to your case.
- This is a general overview, not legal advice. Your actual rights depend on your custody order, state law, and specific circumstances.

## Tips

- This document is a general overview. Your actual rights depend on your specific custody order and your state's laws. Always verify with an attorney.
- If an institution (school, doctor, etc.) is refusing to recognize your parental rights, provide them with a certified copy of your custody order in writing and request a written response.
- Document every violation. A single violation may not move a court to action, but a documented pattern of violations is powerful evidence.
- Know the difference between legal custody (decision-making authority) and physical custody (where the child lives). Your rights may differ depending on your custody arrangement.
- Keep a copy of this reference, your custody order, and your attorney's contact information accessible at all times.
