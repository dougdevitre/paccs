# Child Observation Journal

> **Audience:** Parents, Therapists, GALs, Evaluators
> **Purpose:** Maintain a factual, behavioral record of the child's words, actions, and demeanor across different contexts to help professionals assess the child's wellbeing.
> **Related Skill:** paccs-tech-documentation

---

## How to Use This Template

- Record observations the same day they occur. Contemporaneous notes carry more weight with professionals.
- Write down the child's exact words in quotation marks. Do not paraphrase, interpret, or clean up their language.
- Describe only observable behavior (what you saw and heard), not your interpretation of what the child was feeling or thinking.
- Never show this journal to the child. It is for professional use only.
- Include positive observations. This is a record of the child's overall wellbeing, not a list of problems.

## Tips

- Your most powerful entries are the child's verbatim quotes. Write their exact words immediately, while you remember them clearly.
- Never ask leading questions to generate journal entries. Record what the child says and does naturally.
- Note the context carefully. "Child said 'I don't want to go to Dad's' after a 30-minute phone call with Mom" is very different from an unprompted statement.
- Include entries showing the child happy, engaged, and comfortable with you. This documents your positive relationship.
- Share this journal with your therapist and attorney, not on social media. It is a professional tool, not a public document.
